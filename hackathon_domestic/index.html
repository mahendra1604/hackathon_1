DOMESTIC WASTE MANAGMENT

WASTE MANAGMENT

Domestic waste management involves the collection, transportation, processing, recycling, and disposal of waste generated by households. Proper waste management is essential to minimize environmental impact, promote public health, and conserve resources.

SOURCE SEGREGATION

Separating waste at its origin into categories like recyclables, organic waste, and general waste.
Source segregation, also known as waste sorting or separation at the source, is the practice of categorizing and separating different types of waste at the point of origin, typically within households or businesses. The objective is to facilitate the recycling and proper disposal of waste materials

COMPOSTING

Decomposing organic waste like kitchen scraps and yard waste to produce nutrient-rich compost for soil enrichment.
Composting is a natural process that transforms organic waste into a nutrient-rich soil conditioner. It is an environmentally friendly way to recycle kitchen and garden waste, reducing the amount of organic materials sent to landfills.

Waste-To-Energy

Waste-to-energy (WTE) is a process that involves the conversion of non-recyclable waste materials into usable forms of energy, usually electricity or heat. This approach helps to both manage waste and generate energy, contributing to sustainable waste management practices.

Land-fill disposal

Landfill disposal is a common method for the final disposal of waste materials that cannot be recycled or otherwise recovered. In this process, waste is deposited into designated areas of land, known as landfills. While landfilling is a widely used waste management strategy, it comes with environmental challenges. Waste-Reduction

Waste reduction, also known as waste minimization or source reduction, involves strategies to decrease the generation of waste at its source. This approach focuses on preventing the creation of waste in the first place, thereby lessening the environmental impact associated with the production, use, and disposal of materials
